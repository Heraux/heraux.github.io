Why does the lone pair go equatorial?
In 5-domain systems (trigonal bipyramidal), there are two types of positions:
Equatorial — 3 positions in the "belt," each 120° apart in-plane
Axial — 2 positions at the "poles," 90° from the belt
Equatorial domains have 2 neighbors at 90°
Axial domains have 3 neighbors at 90°
Since lone pairs repel harder (1.25x), they want fewer close neighbors.
Equatorial = fewer 90° contacts = less repulsion = lower energy.
The simulation discovers this — watch the lone pair settle equatorial, not axial.
